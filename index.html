COVID deaths result in rise of orphaned and abandoned pets in India

A few weeks ago, Bengaluru-based animal welfare organisation got to know about Buddy, a five-year-old Indie dog, who had lost his pet parent, an 87-year-old man, to COVID-19. Buddy had no one to look after him and he lay in the courtyard of the house, timid, forlorn and tied to a chain. “It took us a couple of hours to cajole Buddy and earn his trust. He was traumatised, scared and we didn’t know if he had had food since the death of his parent,” founder of the organisation that rescued Buddy

“Often, when pet parents die, their neighbours aren’t willing to feed the pets. Even if they do, it stops after 2-3 days. Recently, we got a request from a woman whose husband and mother-in-law died due to COVID. She had to travel to her hometown and couldn’t take care of their three cats. Since we don’t have a shelter home, we are working closely with feeders who go around feeding these felines. Although we have managed to get a few cats adopted through social media, there are roughly 60-100 cats still looking for a family.”

Animal welfare organisations that were trying to deal with abandoned pets during this time last year are now faced with two issues- abandoned and orphaned pets.
